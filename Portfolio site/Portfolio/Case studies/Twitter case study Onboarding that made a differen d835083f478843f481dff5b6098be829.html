Background

Design

We knew from the research that people dropped off at every screen on the flow, so we wanted to keep it to as few screens as possible. Research also showed that contacts were a great way to find relevance. We also wanted to help people think about Twitter in terms of interests and topics.

In addition, I wanted to add one extra screen that didn‚Äôt actually contribute directly to the ‚Äúinstant timeline,‚Äù but I hypothesized it would help address issues of retention in a more human way (warmth, clarity and explanation). My early ideas (see above) allowed me to gain alignment with the team around testing this extra opening screen.

Key ideas:

  • A welcoming opening line to warm up the app + name personalization.
  • An explanation of what Twitter was and introducing the idea of giving value to users by having content be customized (and of course this was something we used to talk about ads as well).
  • Tweets out of the box, and placed in a kind of timeline-like structure to imply what the app would look like, while highlighting emotion, excitement and feelings you might get using it. (I was trust that people would be able understand the product once they got there, and that they didn‚Äôt need to see actual chrome-perfect Tweets to understand why they would like it.)
  • The button was the most personality a Twitter button had seen.

The interest picker was pre-checked with ‚Äúpopular accounts.‚Äù Popular was a compelling word in research studies.

Following our suggestions was one of the two ways to significantly improve your timeline, which is why we strongly encouraged you to accept all our suggested accounts.

  • We explicitly stated this in the body copy, even using the rare exclamation point for emphasis.
  • The button had more text than usual to ensure clarity about what was happening.
  • We designed the interface so that the default accounts were pre-checked, although users could uncheck them if desired.
  • The concept of 'just for you' became a bit of a mantra for us, highlighting both the value and the customization working behind the scenes.
  • At that time, 'following' was defined with hover text to provide clear definitions
